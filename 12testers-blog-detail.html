Google Play "20 Testers" Policy: The Complete 2025 Survival Guide

Everything you need to know about the Closed Testing requirement, how to avoid the dreaded "Insufficient Engagement" rejection, and get your app published.

Sarah Jenkins

Senior Android Consultant

If you created a personal Google Play Console account after November 13, 2023, you've likely hit a wall: you cannot publish to Production until you run a Closed Test with 20 testers for 14 continuous days.

Why did Google introduce this?

Google Play was flooded with low-quality, broken apps. By forcing individual developers to recruit 20 people, Google ensures that:

  • The app actually installs and opens on different devices.
  • The developer has at least a small network or community.
  • Critical bugs are caught before the public release.

The "Insufficient Engagement" Trap

Simply having 20 emails in your list is not enough. Google tracks daily active usage. If your testers just install the app and never open it again, your application for Production Access will be rejected.

Warning: Bot Farms Don't Work

Many developers try to buy cheap "bot" testers. Google detects these instantly because they often come from the same IP range or device emulators. Using bots can get your entire developer account banned for "High Risk Behavior." Always use real human testers with physical devices.

Step-by-Step: How to Pass

1. Recruit 20+ Testers

Aim for 25 testers. Why? Because if one person uninstalls your app on Day 13, your count drops to 19, and the 14-day timer might reset. Always have a buffer.

2. Encourage Daily Interaction

You need your testers to open the app daily. Sending a push notification or an email reminder to your testing group can help. Google wants to see consistent sessions, screenshots, and crash reports.

3. Gather Feedback

Google asks for a summary of feedback when you apply for production. Don't just say "They liked it." You need meaningful data like "Tester A found a UI bug on Pixel 6."

Conclusion

The 20-tester requirement is a hurdle, but it's passable with the right strategy. Focus on engagement, not just numbers, and ensure your testing group is made of real people.
