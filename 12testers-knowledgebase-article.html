How to Set Up Your Google Group for Closed Testing

Updated 2 days ago By Support Team

Before you can start your 14-day countdown, you must create a way for testers to access your app. Google Play Console manages this via email lists or Google Groups. We strongly recommend using Google Groups as it's more reliable and easier to manage.

Why do I need a Google Group?

Instead of adding 20 individual email addresses to the Play Console manually (which is tedious), you create one Google Group link (e.g., testers@googlegroups.com). You add that single email to your Play Console, and anyone who joins the group automatically gets access to your app.

Note: Without this step, your testers will see an "App not available" or "Item not found" error when they click your link.

Step 1: Create the Group

  1. Go to Google Groups and click Create Group.
  2. Group Name: Enter something recognizable, e.g., "[App Name] Alpha Testers".
  3. Group Email: This will auto-generate. You can customize it if you wish.
  4. Click Next.

Step 2: Configure Permissions (Critical!)

This is where most developers get stuck. You must ensure testers can actually join.

  • Who can join the group: Set this to "Anyone can join". (This allows our testers to join instantly without waiting for your manual approval).
  • Who can view conversations: Set to "Group members".
  • Who can post: Set to "Group managers" (to prevent spam).

Step 3: Add to Play Console

  1. Open your Google Play Console.
  2. Navigate to Testing > Closed testing.
  3. Click Manage track (usually Alpha).
  4. Go to the Testers tab.
  5. Select Google Groups and paste your new group email address (e.g., my-app-testers@googlegroups.com).
  6. Click Save Changes.

Troubleshooting

If testers still report issues:

Common Errors:

  • Error: "App not available for this account"
  • Fix: The user has not joined the Google Group yet. Send them the Group URL first.
